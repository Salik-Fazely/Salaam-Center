A connected foundation

Growing up abroad should not mean growing apart from your heritage.

Many Afghan parents want their children to grow confidently in the country they call home while also maintaining a meaningful connection with their family language, faith and cultural identity. Salaam Center brings these areas together through supportive, age-appropriate online learning.

Language

Help learners strengthen their connection with Dari/Persian and communicate more confidently with family.

Faith

Support a positive and age-appropriate relationship with Quran learning.

Culture

Help children understand and value their Afghan identity and cultural heritage.

Character

Explore practical Islamic manners, values and ethical behaviour in everyday life.

How it works

A calm path into learning

  1. Explore a program

    Review the available formats and learner eligibility.

  2. Attend a free 40-minute trial

    Understand the teaching environment, program and class format.

  3. Receive an initial recommended learning direction

    Discuss an appropriate next step based on the information available.

  4. Confirm the class format and next steps

    For a private program, review the published plans after teacher and schedule availability are confirmed. Group placement follows an available cohort.

Parent involvement

A respectful, supportive learning environment

Salaam Center is designed to provide a respectful, supportive learning environment. Parents and guardians remain involved in communication, scheduling and important decisions for learners under 18.

Parents and guardians remain the primary contact

Scheduling and important learning decisions for children are managed through a parent or guardian. Teacher profiles do not publish personal contact details.
