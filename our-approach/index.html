Our approach

Heritage learning that connects language, faith, culture and character

Salaam Center is designed for Afghan families raising children abroad. Our approach recognises that heritage is not preserved through one subject alone. Language, family connection, faith, culture and character strengthen one another.

Guiding principles

Connection before pressure

Connected learning

Our programs are designed around different but connected parts of Afghan heritage.

Age-appropriate support

Lessons should meet learners at an appropriate stage and encourage steady, positive participation.

Cultural understanding

Learners benefit from educators who understand Afghan families and the experience of growing up between cultures.

Parent involvement

Parents and guardians remain the primary contact for children’s scheduling, communication and important learning decisions.

Encouragement before pressure

The learning environment should build confidence and connection without fear-based or guilt-based teaching.

Safe learning environment

Salaam Center is designed to provide a respectful, supportive learning environment. Parents and guardians remain involved in communication, scheduling and important decisions for learners under 18.

The family’s role

Parents remain central to the learning journey

For learners under 18, a parent or guardian is the primary contact, manages scheduling and remains involved in important learning decisions.

Children can build a confident future while remaining connected to where they come from.
