Free trial information

Start with a free 40-minute trial

The free trial helps families understand the program, class format and recommended next step before choosing a paid plan.

A clear first step

One trial, with no payment or purchase obligation

One free 40-minute trial is available per learner. No payment is required and there is no obligation to purchase a plan.

The conversation helps clarify the learner’s current direction, goals and a suitable program. Trial availability depends on teacher and schedule availability. A specific teacher or time is not guaranteed.

Safe contact for each learner

A parent or guardian is the primary contact for a child or teenager. The parent or guardian should be available at the beginning and end when requested. An adult woman learner may be the primary contact.

Children are not invited to submit personal contact information or contact a teacher privately.

Learner eligibility

Who may attend a trial

The trial uses the same 40-minute duration across the three launch programs.

  • Quran: children aged 5–16 and women of any age
  • Dari/Persian: children aged 5–16 and women of any age
  • Afghan Culture & Islamic Ethics: children and teenagers aged 5–16

After the conversation

What happens next?

A recommendation may be offered, but the next step still depends on teacher, schedule or cohort availability.

  1. Trial and learning discussion

    Discuss the learner’s current direction, goals and suitable program.

  2. Teacher and schedule availability confirmation

    Confirm a suitable private teacher and time, or wait for a published group cohort.

  3. Plan selection

    For a private program, select one plan for one learner and one program.

  4. Review terms and payment information

    Review the complete terms and payment instructions before any payment.

  5. Paid classes begin

    Private paid lessons begin only after the schedule, plan and payment are confirmed.
